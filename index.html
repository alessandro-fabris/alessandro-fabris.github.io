About
Algorithmic fairness Responsible AI Data governance AI Act & DSA Quantification

I am a tenure-track assistant professor at the University of Trieste, where I study algorithmic fairness, responsible AI, and data governance. I measure and operationalize fairness in real-world AI systems, bridging policy (AI Act) and technical evaluation.

I co-founded the European Association for Algorithmic Fairness, which organizes the yearly European Conference for Algorithmic Fairness. I am currently especially engaged with the AI Act, the DSA, and their practical implementation.

My work has been recognised with a Humboldt Research Fellowship, the Elena Cornaro Award for gender studies, the IP&M Best Ph.D. Paper Award, and the ECIR Senior Program Commitee Member Award . My research on algorithmic hiring has been cited by the European Parliament Research Service, and my work on discrimination in car insurance has been featured in national TV (Rai3) and international press (AlgorithmWatch).

Previously, I spent four years in industry at IBM and Electrolux and two years as a postdoc at the Max Planck Institute for Security and Privacy. I enjoy working across disciplines and using mixed methods to tackle complex socio-technical questions; if you're interested in collaborating, feel free to reach out.
